# Submit information and Check answers are peer patterns, not one

The submit-information pattern documents the full validate → review → confirm journey for committing structured information; the check-answers pattern documents the narrower review-and-change-back moment that sits inside it. They share an underlying concept — letting users see and correct what they have entered before they commit — but each teaches a different design decision and has its own focused interactive demo.

The alternative was collapsing both into a single fat Pattern with the contact-details Flow as the only demo. Rejected because the Flow demonstrates three patterns at once (validation-surfaces-at-page-level, check-answers review, confirmation page) and folding them blurs which decision the Pattern is about. Each pattern deserves prose that explains its own trade-offs without competing with two others on the same page. gov.uk takes the same split (their `/patterns/question-pages/` and `/patterns/check-answers/` are peers); Groundwork's broader pattern is renamed to "submit information correctly" because the Flow is a single-page form with review/confirm steps, not gov.uk's one-question-per-page shape.

The trade-off accepted: two Pattern pages share an audience and risk drift; a reader on submit-information may not realise check-answers is its own page with its own focused demo. Mitigated by cross-linking from submit-information's review section, and by giving each Pattern its own dedicated demo (the fat ContactDetailsFlow for the journey; a slim CheckAnswersDemo for the moment).

Re-evaluate if a third pattern emerges that needs its own slice of the submission journey (Question pages, Validation, Confirmation) — at which point this becomes a family of related patterns and a Pattern-family index may be warranted. Also re-evaluate if the cross-linking from submit-information to check-answers proves load-bearing enough that one combined page would read more naturally than two.
